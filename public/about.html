About Me

A little more about where I come from, what drives me, and the things that shaped who I am today.

My Journey

Outside academics, I try to live with balance—spending time with family, improving myself, making music, staying active, and being grateful for opportunities that help me grow as a developer and as a person.

My Creative Side

Music

Music is a huge part of my life. I’ve been a drummer for years, playing everything from church music to funk grooves. Drumming teaches me discipline, rhythm, flow, and how to stay present— all skills that surprisingly translate well into coding.

I love experimenting with gear, recording setups, and digital production. Whenever life gets noisy, drumming is my reset button.

Staying Active

Sports

I’ve always balanced my technical work with physical activity. Football has been one of my favorite sports growing up—it taught me teamwork, awareness, resilience, and the importance of staying grounded both mentally and physically.

Whether it’s training, going for a run, or playing with friends, sports remind me that progress is slow, consistent, and always worth the effort.

Play & Inspiration

Gaming

Gaming is one of the biggest ways I relax and learn at the same time. I enjoy titles that mix strategy, storytelling, and mechanical skill—the same creativity and systems thinking that I apply when building software and games.

I follow platforms like IGN and gaming communities to stay updated on design trends, new releases, and world-building approaches.

Workspace

Desk Setup

I care a lot about my workspace because it affects how I think and create. My setup is tuned for both development and music—comfortable peripherals, clean cable routing, and a layout that lets me switch quickly between coding, XR work, and drumming gear.

I’m always experimenting with small upgrades and layout changes to make the desk more ergonomic, focused, and inspiring for long study or project sessions.

More details
Music Gear

Instruments Rig

Beyond the desk, a big part of my setup lives in the instruments I use. From drum kits to monitoring and recording gear, I like understanding how each piece works together as a system.

I’ve built a small but intentional collection of instruments that support practice, live playing, and recording—from acoustic drums to e-drums with integrated monitoring workflows.
